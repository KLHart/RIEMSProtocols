Acute Neurologic Event with Evidence of Increased ICP
Recognition
  • Patient with altered mental status ( Glasgow Coma Scale < 8), abnormal motor posturing, unilateral or bilateral dilation of pupils, +/- bradycardia and or hypertension
  • Possible etiologies include traumatic brain injury, epidural hematoma, subdural hematoma, subarachnoid hemmerrhage, primary intracerebral hemorrhage, tumor or encephalopathy

Treatment for all providers

  • Routine patient care
  • If feasible, elevate the head of bed to > 30°
  • Avoid obstructions to venous drainage, such as tight cervical collars, securing devices for endotracheal tubes, or a non-midline head position
  • Provide airway management as indicated per the age appropriate:
    • Adult Airway Management Protocol
    • Pediatric Airway Management Protocol
  • Ventilate the patient to maintain an EtCO2 of 30-35 mmHg (avoid over aggressive hyperventilation), if capnography is not available, ventilate the patient at a rate of 14-16 bpm
  • Perform blood glucose analysis and manage hypoglycemia per the age appropriate:
    • Adult Diabetic Emergecies
    • Pediatric Diabetic Emergecies
  • Transport the patient to the nearest appropriate Hospital Emergency Facility
Cardiac/Advanced
  • Manage hypotension per the age appropriate:
    • Adult General Shock and Hypotension Protocol
    • Pediatric General Shock and Hypotension Protocol

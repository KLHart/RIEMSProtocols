Recognition
  • History of AI/Addisonâ€™s disease, HIV/AIDS, sepsis.
  • History of long term use of steroids (asthma, COPD, rheumatoid arthritis, organ transplant), use of antifungal agents
  • Hypotension, nausea, vomiting, dehydration, abdominal pain.

Treatment for all providers

  • Routine patient care
  • Maintain and promote normothermia by the use of blankets and increasing ambient temperature if possible
  • Perform blood glucose (bG) analysis and treat as indicated per the age appropiate:
    • Adult Diabetic Emergecies
    • Pediatric Diabetic Emergecies
  • Provide airway management as indicated per the age appropriate:
    • Airway Management Protocol - Adult
    • Airyway Management Protocol - Pediatric
  • Transport the patient to the nearest appropriate Hospital Emergency Facility.
Cardiac/Advanced/Paramedic
  • HYDROCORTISONE 100 mg (2 mg/kg, 100 mg max for peds) IV/IO or METHYLPREDNISOLONE 125 mg (2 mg/kg, 60 mg max for peds) IV/IO or DEXAMETHASONE 10 mg (0.3 mg/kg, 10 mg max for peds) IV/IO.
  • Manage hypotension/shock per the age appropriate:
    • Adult General Shock and Hypotension Protocol
    • Pediatric General Shock and Hypotension Protocol
